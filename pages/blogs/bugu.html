Development process

One day after entering college, I played Football Manager 2020, a game developed by Sports Interactive. This simulation game allows players to manage their teams, participate in contests, and keep statistics. At this time, an inspiration suddenly appeared in my mind: since I love sports so much, could I bring this virtual game to the virtual campus and provide a sports data service platform for the school? It is one of the inspirations of Bugu Sport. I discovered the infinite possibilities of programming and found a new goal and meaning within myself: I was not satisfied with just being a player, a user. Instead, I would like to be a creator and provider. To achieve this goal, in April, I started the development of Bugu Sport as a team leader.

To achieve this goal, in April, I started the development of Bugu Sport as a team leader.
Step 1: The first step was to recruit good people. I sent invitations to two of my best friends from the same major who also loves soccer because I believe they are interested in combining sports and programming. That passion is essential for the development of the project. I also invited a friend majoring in design to ensure our user interface design was beautiful and reasonable. Finally, I built the team.
Step 2: The project development needed to determine the "direction," and I played the role of a "product manager," conducting product research on the needs of campus sports and collecting user surveys with my teammates for analysis and discussion.
Step 3: I refined the requirements and differentiated them by modules, and assigned each teammate to develop an independent module, such as the game invitation, street-ball organization, team management, and match management, strictly following the rule of high cohesion & low coupling.
Step 4: After each requirement is developed, I will accept the pull request, conduct code review, and use the method of white-box testing to test the software.

However, things were not as smooth as expected. The school's 2020 software Design Competition jury eliminated our work in the preliminary contest, which was a massive blow to the team. For a while, we were in a state of self-doubt. As the team leader, I was not willing to give up easily. I tried to find a solution while comforting my teammates. Through communication with teachers and professors, I discovered our demand analysis and development problems and made improvements. For example, in terms of demand, we have done more questionnaires to expand the scope of respondents; We have also focused our business on organizing and inviting events and teams, reducing unnecessary demands; We changed the Waterfall Development mode into Agile Development mode and adopted stricter code specifications, such as Google JavaScript Style Guide, which improved the readability and extensibility of our code

Through continuous efforts, our program won more and more users' love, and finally, on June 2020, we successfully entered the final 32 teams from more than 4000 participating teams across the country.

Aside from the awards, the whole development process was the most rewarding for us. I learned a lot from this process. In addition to developing technology, I also learned that it is challenging to do an excellent job in a product. Every night I have a hundred different ideas that I argue within my head. I felt a sense of satisfaction every time I completed a requirement, especially when I saw users fall in love with sports and meet more friends because of our app.

All in all, I don't want to stop here. I believe there is still much more to come, especially in the commercial field. In China, there are still many opportunities for commercialization in the development of campus sports. By referring to the NCAA league, I think campus sports in China can also have a platform to provide students with digital services, which can help them meet more people who love sports and fall in love with sports.

If you are also interested, let's promote the development of campus sports together.
