Ideas came

One day after I entered college, I was playing Football Manager 2020, a game developed by Sports Interactive, which is a simulation game that allows players to manage their own teams, participate in games, and keep statistics, and at this time, an inspiration suddenly appeared in my mind: since I love sports so much, could I bring this virtual game to the real campus and provide a sports data service platform for the school? This is one of the inspirations of Bugu Sport. I discovered the infinite possibilities of programming and found a new goal and meaning within myself: I was not satisfied with just being a player, a user, but a creator, a provider.

In order to achieve this goal, in April I started the development of Bugu Sport as a team leader.
Step 1: The first step was to recruit good people. I sent invitations to two of my best friends from the same major who also love soccer, because I believe they are as interested as I am in the idea of combining sports and programming, and that passion is essential for the development of the project, and I also sent an invitation to a friend who is major in design to make sure our user interface design is beautiful and reasonable, so a team of 4 people was formed.
Step 2: the project development needed to determine the "direction", and I played the role of a "product manager", conducting product research on the needs of campus sports, and collecting user surveys with my teammates for analysis and discussion.
Step 3: I refined the requirements and differentiated them by module, and assigned each person to develop an independent module for game invitation, wild ball organization, team management and tournament management, strictly following the principle of high cohesion & low coupling.
Step 4: After each small requirement is developed, I will accept the pull request, conduct code review, and use the method of white-box testing to test the software.
