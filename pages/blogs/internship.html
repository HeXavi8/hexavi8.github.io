Tencent Internship Experience

Share my internship experience.

2021.7.31


I was glad to get the opportunity of a summer internship at Tencent and finally got the offer of full-time employment. I have been working in Tencent Cloud and Smart Industry Group for several months, and I would like to share my internship experience.
I want to share the interview preparation, development technology, and Tencent's life experience in this blog.
Now I am participating in developing the Tencent Coral Marketing Platform, mainly responsible for front-end and full-stack development. If you would like to experience this website, please click the following button.

  • Tencent Coral Marketing Platform

Preparation

🐧 Resume preparation: my resume was done with WonderCV because many resume templates are available there.
🐧 Interview preparation: Niuke front-end guide (key: network, database, operating system, front-end foundation) ➕ LeetCode (easy and medium).
🐧 Interview process:
1. The first interview: mainly focused on databases, networks, operating systems, and the front-end foundation.
2. The second interview: asked about the projects I had worked on and the development process details.
3. HR interview: talk about the business related to the department, the technology used, and the projects I had participated in before.

  8. Sports day: weekly activities of ⚽️, 🏓, 🏀, 🏸 and internal game competition will be organized.
  9. Team building: team building activities every month.

Work Experience

The leaders and mentors in the group are amiable, friendly, and patiently answer all kinds of questions. I learned a lot from them.

The coral marketing platform is mainly responsible for TOB service, helping different companies to carry out marketing services more conveniently and reducing the cost of self-developed software or mini-programs. The primary technology stacks used in the development process are JavaScript, HTML, CSS, Node.js; Vue, React, Koa, Egg; MySQL, MongoDB, Redis; Webpack, Docker, etc.

I was familiar with Javascript, HTML, CSS, VUE, and React, but I still had a lot need to be learned. Besides the front-end technology stack, I still need to know more about the back-end, including Node.js and database knowledge. Although Node.js is based on javascript, many concepts and applications still need to be learned, including Koa and Egg frameworks. Therefore, during the development process of the project, I significantly improved my engineering ability.

At the same time, the connection of the front-end and back-end also needs to study. Axios is one of the vital technology. How to use it to carry out data transmission and what type of transmission data all need to be familiar. The operation of the database is not only "add" and "delete," but also requires me to know deeply about transactions, lock, and such concepts. Besides, I need to learn about network security, such as how to protect against SQL injection, how the front-end protects against XSS attacks, etc.

Also, we need to be skilled in using Git for version management, TAPD for project management, etc.

In the process of practice, I found a big gap between theory and practice. Maybe what we learn in college doesn't all apply in the workplace. For example, how to communicate effectively in the workplace is not taught in school. Therefore, I need to improve my comprehensive ability to suit the job in the industry. All in all, I cherish this internship opportunity, and I did learn a lot.
